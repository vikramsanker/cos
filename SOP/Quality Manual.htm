V.      Quality Management System - Requirements

IV.1     General requirements

IV.1.1       General requirements

a)   Caltek has a well-established QMS implemented at all levels of CALTEK in line with the ISO/IEC 17025, API Specification Q1 (9th Edition) and ISO 9001 standards.

b)   The processes required by the Quality Management System and their application are available throughout the departments. The overall process model of Caltek is documented in Process Map (CT/GEN/QSP) and details the identified processes and their interactions. Individual functional processes and their interactions are documented as Quality System Process (QSP) flow as separate documents and made available in the individual departments.

c)   Resources are provided to ensure that these processes perform effectively to meet the desired objectives of Caltek and as well as customer requirements.

d)   When the planned results are not achieved, suitable corrective actions are taken to bring the process performance to the desired level. When processes have performed consistently to meet the objectives, the process is reviewed to explore the feasibility of setting higher targets to ensure continual improvement.

e)   The support processes like infrastructure maintenance, Software support and Transportation (for collection and delivery of customer instruments) services are outsourced. Outsourced processes are suitably monitored to ensure that Calteks requirements in satisfying the customers are met.

IV.1.2       Confidentiality and Impartiality

The calibration services and its related activities are undertaken impartially and structured and managed to safeguard impartiality. The organization ensures the protection of its customers' confidential information and proprietary rights, including procedures for protecting the electronic storage and transmission of results. For more details, refer procedure for managing impartiality and confidentiality (CT/GEN/CI)

IV.1.3    Quality Policy

The Quality Policy of CALTEK is as under:

We, at CALTEK, are committed to continually provide impartial calibration and measurement services, improve the effectiveness of its quality management system, ensure compliance to the applicable standards and simultaneously achieve the organizational objectives to meet or exceed the customer requirements, whether stated or implied is the quality policy effective from 25-Sep-2019

The Quality Policy is communicated throughout CALTEK and displayed in prominent locations. The Quality policy provides a framework for reviewing quality objectives.

IV.1.5    Planning

The senior management team of CALTEK ensures that the methods and criteria required for the processes of the quality management system through developing the work flow instructions for each department. The effectiveness of the defined process flow is ensured by internal audits. Quality management system planning is carried out through developing any procedures or changes being carried out for the existing procedures. This planning targets fulfillment of the requirements of customer, of CALTEK, and of the quality objectives. This process facilitates maintenance of the integrity of the QMS when changes are planned and implemented.

IV.1.6    Communication

a)            Internal

The management has established the following means of communication within CALTEK to communicate the information regarding the effectiveness of the Quality Management System.

       Inter-office notes and e-mails for correspondence from one to another,

       Verbal communication followed by e-mail under conditions involving urgency,

       Messages on Chat applications and Mobile phones followed by e-mail under conditions involving urgency,

       Display of any major events / achievements and effective performance of the QMS through notice boards,

       Periodic reviews and Open House discussions

The internal communications include but not limited to customer feedback, performance feedback, customer requirements, legal requirements, change in CALTEK`s policy and procedures, changes demanded by the change management procedure and other information required by the organization and management. The responsibility has been assigned to the individual department head who may then delegate the task to their subordinates.

The process communication is illustrated in the process map (CT/GEN/QSP).

b)            External

The CALTEK management has implemented a process for communicating with the customer and/or external organizations to ensure that the requirements are understood throughout all the departments, wherever applicable.

The requirements are received from the customer in the form of purchase order/acknowledged quotation through e-mail or along with the instruments delivered. The requirements then communicated back to the customer for acknowledgment. Please see individual department Quality System Process (QSP) for more details.

The customer representative is contacted through telephone followed by an e-mail about the non-conforming calibration service. The e-mail clearly states the name of the instrument, serial number, customer reference number, model number and the brief description about the nature of non-conformance.

The feedback and customer complaints are inquired and handled by the Quality division. Root cause investigation is conducted by the Senior Manager - Corporate Quality and Information Systems along with the department head to identify the person responsible and process gap for the complaint. The corrective action appropriate to the cause of the complaint is implemented by the department head along with the Senior Manager - Corporate Quality and Information Systems.

IV.2     Management Responsibility

IV.2.1    General

Caltek s senior management is responsible and committed to the development and implementation of the QMS and improving its effectiveness. The management acts on its commitment by establishing the Quality Policy and Quality Objectives, deploying them throughout Caltek at various functions and levels, reviewing the QMS and ensuring resource availability. The Management gives due focus to customer and also communicates the importance of meeting the same in the awareness trainings.

IV.2.2    Legal Responsibility

The Laboratory, which is part of the Company, shall be legally responsible for the scope of work. CALTEK Private Limited is registered in Singapore and shall be legally responsible accordance to the Companies Act, Cap 50 of Singapore.

IV.2.3    Management responsibility away from its permanent facilities

The organization management have its Laboratory staff trained outside its permanent premises to perform the calibration accordance to the ISO/IEC 17025 and customer requirements. These sites may be located at owners facility or other site, either within the building or outdoors. On-site calibration service is available for all parameters specified in the scope of application, unless not stated. The best measurement uncertainty is applicable to on-site calibration, unless the environmental conditions are different from the Laboratory. It is the responsibility of the Laboratory to ensure the conditions are met before undertaking such calibration. If the conditions are different, the Laboratory will perform the necessary correction.

IV.2.4    Conflict of Interest

The Organization has clear policies and responsibilities for key personnel who are performing activities other than calibration.

  The sales department is independently managed and does not have conflict with the calibration department.

  The department is headed by the Director. He ensures that there is no conflict when it comes to calibration of instruments. However, the sales department is also partially managed by the Operations Manager in the absence of the Director.

IV.2.5    Responsibility, authority and accountability

The hierarchy for functional responsibilities and authorities in CALTEK is defined in the organization chart available in Annexure A of this manual. The responsibilities and authorities for the other levels are communicated within businesses and functions.

The Quality System Process & standard operating procedures illustrate functions, their interrelations, responsibilities and authorities. More specific responsibilities and authorities is defined & documented by Roles and Responsibilities. Further to add, the laboratory heads are assigned with the following responsibilities:

       Identify and record any problems relating to the services provided and thereby recommend and/or provide actions to rectify the same.

       Take necessary action to implement and maintain the established quality standards and remain abreast with the latest requirements.

       Monitor the effectiveness of the Quality System and use it as a basis for system improvements.

       Address customer complaints in an efficient manner and initiate the necessary corrective action.

IV.2.6    Managerial and Technical Personnel

(a)    The laboratory has management staff with the experience, knowledge, authority and resources needed to discharge their duties, and the personnel to identify departures from quality system and procedures for performing calibration services. Action plans to be initiated if departures are detected and to minimize such departures.

(b)    The laboratory has arrangements to ensure that its personnel are free from commercial, financial and other external forces, which might adversely affect the quality of their work. The Laboratory staff shall be counseled yearly to ensure that external pressures are resolved. The Laboratory staff will also be required to sign a declaration that they have not accepted any form of gifts and gratuities from customer and not in any form of financial pressures.

(c)     Where relevant all customers confidentiality and propriety rights are protected and stored in a safe place for a period of five years.

(d)    The lab is organized in such a way that confidence in its independence of judgment and integrity is maintained at all times. Staff will be required to confirm their status in their personal records.

(e)    The responsibility, authority and interrelation has been specified and documented for all personnel who manage, perform or verify work affecting the quality of calibrations and tests. (Refer Organization Structure)

(f)     The supervision and training have been provided by qualified and/or experienced personnel familiar with the calibration or test methods and procedures.

(g)    The Technical Management Team and the operations shall be headed by the Technical Manager. The Technical Manager and the team shall ensure that the resources are available to the Calibration Department to ensure smooth operation. The Technical Management Team comprises of the Technical Managers, Asst. Technical Managers and the Laboratory Engineers.

(h)    The laboratory appointed Quality Manager (Management Representative) is responsible for the quality system and its implementation. The Quality Manager reports to the Director of the laboratory to ensure direct access to the highest level of management where decisions are taken on laboratory policy or resources.

(i)     The staff shall be trained in a manner to accept, understand and delegate responsibilities. The senior staff will be evaluated periodically on their contribution, effectiveness and understanding of the system by the management.

(j)     The staff will be briefed on their job responsibilities, documented as Roles and Responsibilities (R&R), and the amendments on the same will be done as and when required.

IV.2.7    Management Representative

Vikram Ayyadurai, Senior Manager - Corporate Quality and Information Systems is appointed as a Management Representative (MR) who, irrespective of other responsibilities, have the authority and responsibility to establish, implement and maintain the QMS effectively. The MR identifies the deviations from the management system or from the procedures for performing laboratory activities through internal audits, feedbacks from customers or interested parties.

HR manager identifies any above said deviations by collecting feedback from employees and the same is shared with the Director. The MR reports the performance of the QMS periodically to the senior management team. The MR also identifies the need for improvement of effectiveness of QMS. Awareness of lab-specific requirements is promoted by the laboratory Heads. The documented and detailed responsibilities, authorities and accountabilities (CT/GEN/R&R/04) are communicated to the MR by the Director.

IV.4     Facilities and Environmental Conditions

The laboratory ensures that all testing and measurement items are maintained under specified conditions by adopting the following practices at all times while performing the calibration.

1.     Record the environmental conditions of each laboratory using a Thermo hygrometer at 10 minutes interval (interval shall be configurable in the meter). The meter data shall be transferred to the server on monthly basis manually. The data shall be maintained by the Quality team, analyzed and circulated to the Technical management for further study.

2.     Maintain sufficient illumination and good housekeeping

3.     Clean all instruments before being brought into the Laboratory to ensure cleanliness of the instrument.

4.     Aircondition the Laboratory to maintain the Temperature and Humidity as specified for the calibration

5.     The environments conditions are monitored for the calibrations that are carried out in the remote location i.e., away from the Caltek`s permanent facility. Refer Onsite-QSP (CT/ONS/QSP) for more details

6.     A spare thermo hygrograph is always maintained and used to monitor the environmental conditions, while the current hygrograph was sent out for calibration or under repair.

For more details, refer procedure for control of monitoring and measuring equipment (CT/GEN/MME)

IV.5     Equipment

All master instruments (reference measurement standards) are subject to periodic calibration from external agencies such as NMC/NIST/NPL. Calibration stickers are attached to the instruments in order to determine the status and all users are instructed on the importance of calibration. These instruments are safe guarded and protected from damage. Certificates received after calibrations are reviewed by technical supervisor to ensure the accuracy of the instruments. Each lab keeps the temp. / Humidity recorder during calibration to provide the evidence of conformity of the process to determined requirements. Refer Procedure for the control of monitoring and measurement for more details (CT/GEN/MME)

IV.6     Metrological Traceability

Caltek establishes and maintains metrological traceability of its measurement results by means of a documented unbroken chain of calibrations, each contributing to the measurement uncertainty, linking them to an appropriate reference. For more details, refer procedure for control of monitoring and measuring equipment (CT/GEN/MME)

IV.7     Documentation Requirements

IV.4.1       General

Caltek` s documentation is four level structure.

Level I The Quality Policy, Quality Objectives and Scope of the organization

Level II The Quality manual consisting of adopted quality management system, reference to the mandatory procedures required by the standard, process model of Caltek and description of Calteks management system.

Level III - The technical procedures for performing the calibration and its associated processes are considered under this level. This level also refers to the Quality System Process (QSP) which details the work flow processes of individual functions/labs. The Standard Operating Procedures (SOP) including Instruction Manual are also considered under this level. These provide instructions or guidelines to perform an activity.

Level IV The documents and records that include reports, checklists ensure effective planning, operation and control of processes and provide evidence of conformity to requirements of CALTEK / Customer / Statutory and Regulatory bodies / International standard. Refer Procedure for Control of Records (CT/GEN/CR) and Control of Documents (CT/GEN/CD) for more details.

IV.4.2       Quality Manual

This Quality Manual, established and maintained in CALTEK and includes the scope of the QMS with exclusions and their justifications, references to the mandatory procedures required by the standard. A description of the interaction between the processes of the quality management system is documented as process-inter-relationship diagram (Process Map). Refer high level process map (CT/GEN/QSP).

IV.4.3       Control of Documents

CALTEK ensures that all the documents as required by the Quality Management System are controlled. A documented procedure has been established, which defines the controls to ensure:

       Preparation, review, approval for adequacy prior to use, issue, update when required, review, update, re-approve, re-issue of documents

       Identification of changes and present revision status of documents

       Availability of relevant versions of documents at points of use

       Identification and legibility of documents

       Application of controls to documents of external origin like Standards, Drawings, Legal Documents, Licenses, Documents of Statutory and Legal Requirements etc.

       Prevention of inadvertent use of obsolete documents and their identification if retained.

       Control of the documents and the concerned responsibilities and authorities. The availability mode and retention period of all the documents are mentioned in the List of documents and records (CT/../LDR) of the individual department.

Further details are given in the Procedure for Control of Documents (CT/GEN/CD). The contracts with the customers are confidential and are maintained by the individual sales person. The guidelines for filing the contracts are available in the contracts file itself. The license copies of any documents that are purchased by CALTEK are kept confidential and restricted to the relevant personnel only.

IV.4.4       Control of Records

In order to establish evidence of conformity to requirements and the effective operation of the quality management system, records are generated and maintained by various departments and sections. These records are the evidence for systematic quality management in operation. These records are reviewed periodically to identify the trend and weaknesses in the system and hence to initiate actions to prevent them.

A documented procedure has been established to define controls needed for the identification, storage, protection, retrieval, retention and disposition of records and to ensure that records remain legible, readily identifiable and retrievable.

The availability mode and retention period of all the documents are mentioned in the List of documents and records (CT/../LDR) of the individual department. Further details are given in the Procedure for Control of Records (CT/GEN/CR).

IV.4.5       Technical records

All records (including those pertaining to calibration measurement and test equipment), log books, computer data files, certificates and reports shall be safely stored, held securely and held in confidence to the customer. The calculations and completeness of a calibration shall be reviewed and signed or initialed by the person in charge of the laboratory or his/her designate. The records for each calibration and test shall contain sufficient information to permit their repetition. Further details are given in the Procedure for Control of Records (CT/GEN/CR)

IV.4.6       Complaints

Customer Complaints are attended immediately and appropriate corrective action are taken after an investigation. The report of each complaint is recorded, indicating the corrective action needed to prevent recurrence. Any complaints that are received by any of the department in the organization is forwarded to their immediate supervisor and then to the Senior manager Corporate Quality and Information Systems by the supervisor. The complaints are recorded in Audit findings template (CT/QAS/FOR/01). The following steps are taken when complaints are received.

The client representative informs the customer of the corrective action and handles the necessary follow-up required. In addition to prompt complaint-handling procedures, the customers are sent out with customer feedback survey to gather feedback from customers. For more details, refer procedure for customer satisfaction and data analysis (CT/GEN/CSD) and customer complaints handling process (CT/GEN/QSP/01).

IV.4.7       Control of data and information management

The laboratory information management system used for collection, processing, recording, reporting, storage or retrieval of data are validated for functionality (including functioning of interfaces). The information systems are validated for respective interfaces and functionality, whenever changes are made to the software configuration. The changes are authorized, documented and validated before implementation. For more details, refer procedure for managing information systems (CT/GEN/IS).

V.7      Production and Service Provision

V.7.1   General

The calibration process performed by the lab technicians is considered production in CALTEK. The job receipt is created by the sales or logistic personnel with the target date based on the types of services such as Normal, Semi-Express and Express. The normal service takes 04-07 business days, semi-express takes 03-04 business days and Express service takes 01 business day to turn around the job.

A wide range of software is available for calibration of various instruments. These software applications are periodically updated by the suppliers and it is ensured that the revisions are available in the laboratory for applications. Whenever the software is not available for calibration, instructions are established by the organization taking into consideration the scope of calibration. These established procedures are subject to review by technical personnel to ensure adequacy of the processes.

The Laboratory establishes the procedures and the methods, which are traceable to National or International Standards. All the relevant materials for the proper operation and handling of the instruments are documented, followed and stored in a safe place. The calibration methods and procedures are up to date and made accessible for the relevant personnel. Refer procedure for production and service provision for more details (CT/GEN/PSP).

V.7.3      Identification and Traceability (Handling of calibration items)

All instruments are logged into the software at the time of receipt, and a unique number is allotted to each job, where each job may contain many instruments of same customer. The job number will be tagged on each instrument for identification. The job number establishes traceability to records, laboratory in which the calibration is carried out and all other relevant data. Calibration stickers containing the certificate number will be affixed on the instrument after the calibration process. The organization ensures the identification and traceability of the whole process from input to output throughout product realization. The organization allocates unique identification number for the traceability of reference instruments and maintains the records for the same.

Refer procedure for identification and traceability for more details (CT/GEN/IAT)

CALTEK ensures that customer instruments are safeguarded by always keeping the instruments on the rack. Refer procedure for identification and Traceability for more details (CT/GEN/IAT)

V.9      Control of Non-Conforming Output

If a Non-conformance calibration is identified the work is immediately stopped and the Technical Manager is informed.

       The Technical Manager will evaluate the situation and inform the Quality Manager.

       The Nonconforming product is placed in a designated area.

       The client is informed of the Non-conformance calibration and all the items calibrated from the last Internal Reference Comparison will be recalled for re-calibration, if the non-conformance is due to our Reference Instrument.

       The Technical Manager will identify the problem to detect the source of Non-conformance. He will then inform the Quality Manager to authorize the resumption or work.

       Suitable corrective actions for the reported non-conformity will be taken to prevent recurrences. Refer Procedure for managing non-conforming services and CAPA for more details (CT/GEN/NCP)

VII.  Monitoring, Measurement, Analysis and Improvement of QMS

VI.1     General

CALTEK plans and implements the monitoring, measurement, analysis and improvement processes required to demonstrate the conformity of the product, to ensure the conformity of the QMS and continually improve the effectiveness of the QMS. CALTEK also determines the appropriate methodology including statistical techniques needed for the above processes. Refer Procedure for Customer Satisfaction and data analysis for more details (CT/GEN/CSD).

VI.2.1    Internal Audit

Internal audits are conducted once in a year to verify if the QMS conforms to planned arrangements in line with the requirements of the customers, of CALTEK and the ISO 9001: 2015 and API Spec Q1 (09th Edition) standards and also confirm the effectiveness of implementation and maintenance of the QMS.

       The audit program is planned considering the status and importance of the processes, areas to be audited and the results of previous audits. The auditors conducting the audits are selected, trained and qualified to ensure the objectivity and impartiality of the audit process. Auditors do not audit their own processes.

       The lab-ICs and Laboratory Heads are responsible for the implementation of correction and corrective actions necessary to plug gaps identified during audits.

       A documented procedure has been established to define the responsibilities and requirements for planning and conducting audits, reporting results, taking action for audit closure, reviewing by the management and maintaining the relevant records. Records include audit schedules, audit reports with details of corrections, corrective actions and verifications done.

For Further details are given in the Procedure for Internal Audit.

VI.2.2    Processes Evaluation

The organization applies suitable methods for monitoring and, where applicable, measurement of QMS process. These methods demonstrate the ability of the processes to achieve planned results. When planned results are not achieved, corrective action is taken, as appropriate.

Appropriate process measurements and targets are defined during the management review meetings and are incorporated in different procedures, as appropriate. The process measurements data is collected and reviewed. The data is reviewed against the planned results and is presented to the senior management team using statistical tools like bar charts, pie charts etc. These statistical reports are reviewed in the following management review meeting to initiate suitable corrective actions like resetting of targets, inclusion of new process measures or any other appropriate action. The details of actions taken shall be recorded in Management review meeting minutes.

When determining suitable method, the organization considers the type and extent of monitoring or measurement appropriate to each of its process in relation to their impact on the conformity as required and on the effectiveness of the QMS.

i.   Monitoring and measurement of service

The organization monitors and measures the characteristics of the purchased master instrument to verify that the requirements have been met. Evidence of the conformity with the acceptance criteria are maintained for all reference measurement standards. All instruments received for repair/calibration are subject to inspection. In case, the test instrument is identified to be faulty, the customer will be informed of the status followed by a service report will be issued. The delivery notes for the work completed instrument consists of the person authorizing for release and the person authorizing for receiving the instruments. The delivery of instruments to customer shall not proceed until the planned arrangements have been satisfactorily completed, otherwise approved by a relevant authority and where applicable by the customer.

VI.2     Data Analysis

To demonstrate the suitability and effectiveness of the implemented QMS and to evaluate opportunities for continual improvement of the effectiveness of the QMS, CALTEK determines, collects and analyses appropriate data including that from monitoring and measurement processes. Refer Procedure for Customer Satisfaction and data analysis for more details (CT/GEN/CSD)

VI.3     Improvement

VI.4.1       General

CALTEK continually improves the effectiveness of its QMS by adherence to its Quality Policy and Quality Objectives as well as through Audits, Analysis of Process data, Corrective & Preventive actions and Management reviews. The processes to be focused on for improvement are decided by the management. Improvement efforts will be more on processes that are relatively new and are of strategic importance than on well-established processes.

VI.4.2       Corrective Action

CALTEK takes corrective actions to eliminate the cause of the non-conformities and prevent recurrence. Corrective actions can be taken based on customer feedback/audits. These corrective actions are taken based on the effects of the non-conformities encountered.

If the corrective action requires changes in any documentation, the same is completed. The effectiveness of the corrective actions will be reviewed. Further details are given in the Procedure for Control of Non-conforming output, Corrective Action and Preventive Action.

VI.4.3       Preventive Action

CALTEK takes preventive actions to prevent the occurrence of potential non-conformities. Preventive actions can be taken based on experiences during the pilot, past experiences or based on anticipated issues or errors.

These preventive actions are commensurate with the effects of the potential problems. Refer Procedure for Control of Non-conforming output, Corrective and Preventive Actions for more details.

VI.4     Management Responsibility

VI.5.1       Management Review

The management review shall be conducted at least every 12 months. The review frequency of 12 months shall strictly be based on date of previous review. The management reviews CALTEKs QMS to ensure the continuing suitability, adequacy and effectiveness of the implemented Quality Management System. Records related to the review including decisions taken and action points are maintained and circulated to those concerned.

The review includes assessing opportunities for improvement and the need for changes to the quality management system. The reviews are recorded and maintained as per record retention period.

VI.5.2       Review Input

The input to these reviews includes:

       the status of actions from previous management reviews

       changes in external and internal issues that are relevant to the quality management system

       customer satisfaction and feedback from relevant interested parties Conformity of service output

       Process performance and conformity of services

       monitoring and measurement results

       Audit results

       Status of Quality Objectives

       the performance of external providers

       Status of Corrective and Preventive actions

       the adequacy of resources

       Changes which could impact the QMS

       Recommendations / opportunities for improvement

       the effectiveness of actions taken to address risks and opportunities

VI.5.3       Review Output

The output from these reviews includes actions and decisions but not limited to:

       Improvement of the effectiveness of the QMS and its processes

       Improvement of service related to client requirements

       Resource needs

Caltek establishes, documents, implements and maintains a management system that is capable of supporting and demonstrating the consistent achievement of requirements of ISO/IEC 17025:2017 and assures the quality of the laboratory results. Additionally, Caltek has an implemented quality management system in accordance to the requirements of ISO 9001:2015.
